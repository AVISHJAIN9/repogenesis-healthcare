Your Lungs

The lungs are the centerpiece of your respiratory system, taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide to keep the body functioning.

Chronic Cough

A cough that lingers for more than three weeks, often producing mucus (phlegm).

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing or feeling like you can't get enough air (Dyspnea), even while resting.

Wheezing

A high-pitched whistling sound made while breathing, indicating narrowed airways.

Smoking

Cigarette smoking is the leading cause of lung cancer and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Pollution & Toxins

Long-term exposure to air pollution, asbestos, or industrial chemicals damages lung tissue.

Infections

Repeated respiratory infections like bronchitis or pneumonia can scar the lungs over time.

Asthma

A chronic condition where airways narrow and swell, producing extra mucus and causing breathing difficulty.

COPD

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease, a group of lung diseases that block airflow and make it difficult to breathe.

Pneumonia

An infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, filling them with fluid.

Prevention & Care

Protecting your lungs is vital for long-term health and quality of life.

  • Don't Smoke
    If you smoke, quitting is the best thing you can do for your lungs.
  • Avoid Pollutants
    Avoid secondhand smoke, chemicals, and check air quality index (AQI).
  • Exercise
    Aerobic activity improves lung capacity and efficiency.
  • Checkups
    Regular spirometry tests can detect lung issues early.
