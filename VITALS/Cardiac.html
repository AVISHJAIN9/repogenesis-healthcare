The Human Heart

The heart is a muscular organ roughly the size of a fist, pumping blood through the network of arteries and veins called the cardiovascular system.

Chest Discomfort

Pain, tightness, or pressure in the chest (Angina) is the most common sign of heart danger.

Shortness of Breath

Feeling winded during light activity or while lying down can indicate heart failure.

Palpitations

The feeling of a racing, fluttering, or skipping heartbeat (Arrhythmia).

Fatigue

Unexplained weakness or extreme tiredness can be a symptom of heart inefficiency.

Lifestyle Factors

High cholesterol diet, smoking, lack of exercise, and excessive alcohol consumption.

High Blood Pressure

Uncontrolled hypertension damages arteries, leading to blockages and heart strain.

Genetics

Family history plays a significant role in the risk of developing coronary artery disease.

Coronary Artery Disease

The buildup of plaque in the heart's arteries that can lead to heart attack.

Heart Failure

A chronic condition where the heart doesn't pump blood as well as it should.

Arrhythmia

Improper beating of the heart, whether too fast (tachycardia) or too slow (bradycardia).

Prevention & Care

Small changes in your daily routine can have a massive impact on your heart's longevity.

  • Stay Active
    Aim for 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.
  • Heart-Healthy Diet
    Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats.
  • Quit Smoking
    Smoking chemicals damage the heart and blood vessels.
  • Manage Stress & Sleep
    Chronic stress and poor sleep are linked to heart disease.
