The Science of the Gut

Gastroenterology focuses on the digestive tract and its associated organs: the liver, pancreas, and gallbladder. We diagnose and treat all related conditions.

Chronic Heartburn (GERD)

Frequent acid reflux that irritates the lining of the esophagus, requiring lifestyle changes and expert medical management.

Bowel Changes

Persistent diarrhea, constipation, or alternating patterns often point to Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) or other functional disorders.

Unexplained Abdominal Pain

Acute or chronic discomfort in the stomach area that may be linked to gallstones, ulcers, or inflammatory conditions.

Trouble Swallowing (Dysphagia)

Difficulty or pain when moving food from the mouth to the stomach, often requiring an endoscopy for diagnosis.

Upper Endoscopy

A procedure using a flexible scope to examine the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum for ulcers or inflammation.

Colonoscopy

Essential screening to detect and remove polyps, preventing colorectal cancer. Recommended for all adults over 45.

pH Monitoring

Tests to measure acid exposure in the esophagus, crucial for diagnosing and managing Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD).

Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)

Chronic inflammation involving the digestive tract, including **Crohn's Disease** and **Ulcerative Colitis**.

Hepatitis & Liver Disease

The diagnosis and management of viral hepatitis, cirrhosis, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD).

Peptic Ulcers

Sores that develop on the lining of the esophagus, stomach, or small intestine, often due to **H. pylori** or NSAIDs.

Wellness & Digestive Health

Taking proactive steps is crucial for maintaining a healthy digestive system and preventing chronic illness.

  • Stay Hydrated
    Drinking enough water is essential for optimal digestion and preventing constipation.
  • Increase Fiber Intake
    Foods rich in fiber (fruits, vegetables, whole grains) keep the bowels regular and healthy.
  • Support Gut Flora
    Incorporate probiotics and fermented foods to maintain a balanced microbiome.
  • Limit Alcohol & Caffeine
    Excessive consumption can irritate the stomach lining and worsen reflux symptoms.
