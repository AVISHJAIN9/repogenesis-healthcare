What is Cancer?

Cancer is a complex group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. If not checked, it can result in death.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Losing 10 pounds or more without trying can be an early sign, often associated with pancreas, stomach, or lung cancers.

Persistent Cough

A cough that doesn't go away or becomes hoarse can be a warning sign for lung or throat cancer.

Lumps or Thickening

Can be felt through the skin, particularly in the breast, testicle, lymph nodes, or soft tissues.

Chronic Fatigue

Extreme tiredness that doesn't get better with rest, often an early symptom of leukemia or colon cancer.

Tobacco Use

The leading cause of cancer and death from cancer. It causes lung, mouth, throat, and many other types.

Radiation

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun and ionizing radiation (X-rays) can damage DNA.

Genetics

Inherited genetic mutations play a major role in about 5 to 10 percent of all cancers.

Carcinoma

Begins in the skin or tissues that line internal organs. (e.g., Lung, Breast, Colon).

Sarcoma

Begins in bone, cartilage, fat, muscle, blood vessels, or other connective tissue.

Leukemia

Starts in blood-forming tissue such as the bone marrow and causes large numbers of abnormal blood cells.

Prevention & Care

While not all cancers are preventable, lifestyle choices can significantly reduce your risk.

  • Avoid Tobacco
    Smoking is linked to various types of cancer, including lung, mouth, throat, pancreas, and bladder.
  • Healthy Diet
    Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Limit processed meats.
  • Vaccination
    Protect yourself against Hepatitis B and Human Papillomavirus (HPV).
  • Regular Screenings
    Early detection through self-exams and screenings greatly increases chances of successful treatment.
